SIH2023

Six months ago, I was struck by an idea that led me on an incredible journey. My team and I decided to integrate IoT, an app, a hardware unit, and machine learning into a cohesive prototype. Despite a tight budget and no external funding, we pooled our resources and developed the project ourselves, running from department to department seeking support. Without a confirmed ticket, we made our way to Punjab, determined to showcase our hard work. Competing against 3000 teams, we were thrilled to finish in the top 2. The intense three months of development, coding, and real-life application were worth every effort. I’m grateful to my friends Harish, Barsa, Somyashree, and Chiranjeeb for their invaluable contributions. Special thanks to Ashish Bhaiya, Satyabrat, and Sahi, who supported us throughout this journey. This experience taught us the power of perseverance and teamwork

AICTE-IIM journey

Another significant journey in my life was participating in the IIM Kolkata IDE Boot Camp 2024. This event taught me the art of public speaking and pushed me beyond my comfort zone. Though it wasn't my first time on stage, presenting my portfolio-developed prototype to an audience of 300 was daunting. Competing against India's top 30 teams from institutions like IIIT, NIT, and IIT was incredibly challenging, especially since I came from a core background and they were mostly MTech students in non-core fields. When I introduced myself and my college, Odisha University of Technology and Research, Bhubaneswar, many were surprised and didn't recognize the name. Their reaction made me feel a bit demotivated. While other participants had teams of 4-5 members, I was alone. The continuous three-day ideathon was intense, and I often doubted myself, thinking I might not win. However, when the results were announced, I was shocked and thrilled to find that my hard work had paid off. This experience taught me that dedication and perseverance can lead to success, even when the odds are against you. It made me realize that I have much more to learn, but also that I've achieved something significant, proving that with effort, one can rise from obscurity to accomplishment.
